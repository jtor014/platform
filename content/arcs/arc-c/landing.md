# Arc C: The Content Platform

## The client

A neighbourhood community association. Communicates through a Facebook group but not everyone is on Facebook and content gets buried. Needs their own publication site. Four writers, none technical.

## The story

You start building a publishing tool for people who have never seen a code editor and never will. AI will immediately want to over-engineer it — CMS frameworks, rich text editors, plugin systems. Your job is to keep fighting scope back to what four non-technical writers actually need. By Project 5, you're moderating comments, handling abuse reports, and deciding what happens when someone posts something offensive at 11pm on a Friday. This arc teaches anti-scope discipline harder than any other.

## What you'll build

- **Project 3:** Content management system for non-technical writers — WYSIWYG editor, drafts, scheduled publishing
- **Project 4:** Newsletter with email subscription — double opt-in, delivery verification
- **Project 5:** Comments with moderation and members-only content — abuse prevention, content gating

## What you'll learn that's unique

- Anti-scope discipline — content platforms are where AI over-engineers most aggressively
- Non-technical user acceptance criteria: the "grandmother test" — would a non-technical person understand this?
- Rich text editor verification: does it actually work like a word processor?
- Content moderation and abuse response: what happens when someone posts something offensive?
- Email deliverability: why emails land in spam and how to fix it

## Best for

Digital media, communications, or community development students. Anyone who wants to build for people who don't know what HTML is.

## What makes this arc hard in practice

Saying no. AI will want to build a full CMS framework with plugins, themes, and customisable layouts. Your job is to keep fighting scope back to what four non-technical writers actually need. If you struggle with anti-scope discipline, this arc will either teach you or break you.

## How you'll verify

The "grandmother test." Could a non-technical writer use every feature without help? Open the editor, try to write an article, try to add an image, try to schedule it. If anything requires knowing what a "slug" is or what "Markdown" means, the acceptance criteria weren't specific enough.

## Estimated time

4-6 weeks for Projects 3-5.
