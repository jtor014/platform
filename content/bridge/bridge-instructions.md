# Bridge to Project 3: Preparing to Plan

Projects 1 and 2 gave you the loop: branch, direct, verify, merge. But someone else wrote the PRDs for those projects. From Project 3 onwards, you write them yourself — which means you need to know what a good PRD looks like, what a bad one looks like, and how to turn a vague client brief into something precise enough for AI to build from. That's what this bridge teaches, in three steps: annotate, repair, generate.

---

## Step 1: Annotate (15-20 minutes)

Go back to the two PRDs you've already worked with:
- [GreenScape PRD](/web-dev/projects/1/prd) (Project 1 — static site)
- [FactFeed PRD](/web-dev/projects/2/prd) (Project 2 — dynamic app)

For each PRD, write a one-sentence annotation next to each major section:

| Section | Your annotation should answer |
|---|---|
| Executive Summary | What does this section accomplish in 2-3 sentences? |
| Problem Statement | How does this connect the PRD to the real client need? |
| Scope | How are features described? What makes a good feature description? |
| Anti-Scope | Why is each item listed? What would happen if it wasn't here? |
| Acceptance Criteria | What makes these testable? How is "good" different from "vague"? |
| User Stories | How do these connect to features? |
| Success Metrics | How are these different from acceptance criteria? |

**What you're building:** A mental model of what a PRD contains and why each section matters. You'll use this model when reviewing the AI-generated PRD in Step 2 and generating your own in Step 3.

**By the end of Step 1:** You should have written annotations next to every major section of both PRDs. If you can explain why each section exists and what makes it effective, you're ready for Step 2.

**Specific things to notice:**
- The anti-scope in Project 1 lists 10+ specific items. Each one is something AI would try to add.
- The acceptance criteria in Project 2 include specific HTTP status codes (200, 404) and exact JSON shapes. This is what "testable" looks like for an API.
- Both PRDs have "Open Questions" — things that were unclear rather than assumed.

## Step 2: Repair (30-45 minutes)

You'll receive a flawed PRD specific to your chosen arc. This PRD was generated by AI from a simple client brief — and it has realistic problems that AI commonly produces.

**Your job: find every flaw and fix it.**

Each flawed PRD contains 5-6 problems from this list:
- **Scope creep:** Features the client never asked for
- **Vague acceptance criteria:** "Should be user-friendly" instead of specific, testable criteria
- **Missing anti-scope:** No anti-scope section, or one that's too vague
- **Over-engineered architecture:** Technology recommendations that are overkill for the brief
- **Oversized ticket:** A ticket that's actually 3-4 tickets crammed together
- **Hallucinated library:** A recommended tool or library that doesn't exist or is unmaintained
- **Developer language in user-facing features:** Technical jargon where the user would see it
- **Missing edge cases:** No criteria for invalid input, empty states, or boundary conditions

**How to do the repair:**
1. Read the flawed PRD from start to finish
2. For each section, ask: "Does this match the client brief? Is anything added?"
3. Mark every flaw you find with a comment: what's wrong and what the fix should be
4. Rewrite the flawed sections

**Repair checklist — check each area:**
- Scope: features the client didn't ask for?
- Anti-scope: missing entirely, or too vague?
- Acceptance criteria: vague verbs like "should handle," "works correctly," "is user-friendly"?
- Ticket sizing: any ticket that's really 3-4 tickets in one?
- Architecture: technology that's overkill for the brief?
- Libraries: any tool or library you can't find in the actual docs?

**By the end of Step 2:** You should have a marked-up copy of the flawed PRD with every flaw identified and a fix described. Check your findings against the answer keys on the [Choose Your Arc](/web-dev/bridge/choose-arc) page.

**Don't skip this step.** The repair exercise builds the exact skill you'll use in Step 3 — reviewing AI output critically. It's easier to spot flaws in someone else's work than to avoid them in your own.

---

## Step 3: Generate (45-60 minutes)

Now generate your own PRD. You have:
- The client brief for your arc's Project 3
- The PRD template (`prd-template.md`)
- Experience reading two good PRDs (Step 1)
- Experience fixing a bad one (Step 2)

**Process:**
1. Paste the client brief and the PRD template into conversational AI
2. Let AI generate the full PRD
3. Review every section against the brief:
   - Did AI add features not in the brief? → Remove them
   - Are acceptance criteria vague? → Make them specific
   - Is the anti-scope comprehensive? → Add anything AI would typically assume
   - Is the architecture appropriate for the scale? → Simplify if over-engineered
4. Iterate: tell AI to fix the issues you found, then review again
5. When you're satisfied, the PRD is approved — move to the architecture template

**The quality bar:** Your PRD should be as precise as the two you annotated in Step 1. If your acceptance criteria say "should work well on mobile," go back and make them specific.

**When the PRD is approved, your next artefact is the architecture.** Use the architecture template to produce the technical design, database schema, API contracts, and ticket backlog — then review those with the same rigour.
